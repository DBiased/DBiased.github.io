# DBiased Component Specs

These component rules are derived from the current `dbiased.com` homepage and are intended to keep future pages visually consistent.

## 1. Hero

Purpose:
- Establish the brand immediately
- Communicate one clear market statement
- Anchor the page with color and motion

Structure:
- Full-bleed background
- One centered headline or looping status line
- One supporting action, usually a form or button

Rules:
- Keep the hero centered vertically and horizontally
- Use a high-contrast text color against a warm background
- Keep the copy short
- Use ambient animation, not busy motion

## 2. Button

Preferred style:
- Filled dark violet background
- Warm orange label text
- 2px border or hard-shadow treatment
- Small radius, not pill-only by default

States:
- Default: strong contrast
- Hover: slightly brighter fill or a subtle lift
- Focus: visible violet focus ring
- Disabled: lower opacity, no motion

## 3. Input

Preferred style:
- Pale cream or white fill
- Dark violet border
- Clear placeholder copy
- Enough internal padding to feel calm, not cramped

Rules:
- Inputs should be legible at a glance
- Keep focus states obvious
- Use the same radius as buttons or slightly larger

## 4. Card

Preferred style:
- Flat or lightly lifted surface
- Bold border
- Warm or cream background
- Tight title hierarchy

Rules:
- Cards should not become generic dashboard tiles
- Use cards for signal, not for decoration
- Keep internal spacing compact and intentional

## 5. Tag / Chip

Preferred style:
- Small uppercase or title-case labels
- Violet outline or violet fill
- Warm or cream text treatment depending on contrast

Rules:
- Use tags for labels, categories, and state indicators
- Keep them concise

## 6. Data Block

Preferred style:
- Large numeric value
- Short label
- Optional trend indicator

Rules:
- Prioritize readability
- Keep the layout compact
- Use mono only when precision matters

## 7. Background Motion

Preferred style:
- Slow gradient sweep
- Minimal loops
- No aggressive parallax

Rules:
- Motion should support the page, not compete with it
- If motion is reduced, the layout should still feel complete

## Do

- Use orange, gold, and violet as the foundation
- Use Exo 2 for a consistent brand feel
- Use bold borders and crisp contrast
- Use short, confident language

## Don't

- Do not switch to a muted SaaS palette
- Do not use a glassmorphism treatment
- Do not over-round every control
- Do not add decorative clutter without a functional reason
